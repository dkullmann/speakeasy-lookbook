For the Dames

Her Looks

Channel the bold spirit of the flapper — sequins that catch the gaslight, fringe that sways to the Charleston, and an attitude that says you answer to nobody.

01

The Flapper

The quintessential speakeasy silhouette. A dropped-waist beaded or sequined dress with swaying fringe that catches every flicker of candlelight. Pair with a jeweled headband and T-strap heels for maximum Charleston potential.

  • Sequined or beaded fringe dress
  • Feathered or jeweled headband
  • Long strand of pearls
  • T-strap or Mary Jane heels
  • Finger-wave or bobbed hairstyle
02

The Vamp

For the woman who commands the room in silence. A floor-length bias-cut velvet gown in deep burgundy or black, draped with a faux-fur stole. A pendant necklace catches the light, a long cigarette holder completes the mystique.

  • Floor-length velvet or satin gown
  • Faux-fur stole or feather boa
  • Cloche hat or jeweled turban
  • Art Deco pendant necklace
  • Dark lip & smokey eye
03

The Mob Wife

Power dressing, Capone-era style. She doesn't ask permission — she arrives draped in a lavish fur coat over a sleek black dress, gold dripping from her ears and throat. Dark lips, darker attitude. The don's wife doesn't blend in; she owns the room.

  • Oversized faux-fur coat or stole
  • Sleek black or deep red dress beneath
  • Chunky gold choker & drop earrings
  • Bold dark lip & winged liner
  • Cocktail rings & chain-link bracelet
04

The Billie

Channel the queen of jazz herself. A slim, floor-length satin gown in ivory or champagne, elbow-length gloves, and a single white gardenia pinned in swept-up hair. Understated elegance that says you don't need to shout — you sing.

  • Floor-length satin column gown
  • Elbow-length evening gloves
  • White gardenia hair flower
  • Simple gold stud earrings
  • Soft smokey eye & nude lip
For the Gents

His Looks

Whether you're the smooth-talking bootlegger or the sharp-dressed jazz cat, the 1920s gentleman knows that every detail counts — from the crease in his trousers to the tilt of his hat.

05

The Bootlegger

The sharpest suit in the joint. A navy or charcoal pinstripe three-piece with wide peaked lapels, burgundy silk tie, and a pocket square folded just so. Top it with a tilted fedora and two-tone Oxfords. You run this place.

  • Three-piece pinstripe suit
  • Wide peaked lapels
  • Silk tie & pocket square
  • Fedora or newsboy cap
  • Two-tone Oxford or Wingtip shoes
06

The Jazz Cat

Easy, effortless, but unmistakably of the era. A crisp dress shirt with sleeves rolled to the elbow, gold-hued suspenders, and high-waisted wide-leg trousers. A bow tie and pocket watch chain say you've got rhythm — and style to spare.

  • Crisp dress shirt, rolled sleeves
  • Suspenders & high-waist trousers
  • Bow tie or loose silk tie
  • Pocket watch on chain
  • Slicked-back hair with pomade

The Details

Finishing Touches

The difference between a costume and a character is in the details. These accents transform your outfit into a living, breathing piece of the 1920s.

🪶

Headpieces

Jeweled headbands, feathered fascinators, cloche hats, or a classic fedora tilted at an angle.

💎

Jewelry

Long pearl strands, Art Deco brooches, cocktail rings, and cufflinks with geometric motifs.

🧤

Gloves

Opera-length satin gloves for her, leather driving gloves for him. The ultimate power move.

👞

Footwear

T-strap heels or Mary Janes for the ladies. Two-tone Oxfords or polished Wingtips for the gents.

🎩

Props

Cigarette holders, vintage compacts, feather fans, flasks engraved with your initials, and opera glasses.

✨

Makeup

Dark berry lips, kohl-rimmed eyes, thin penciled brows. Bold and unapologetic — the 1920s way.

The Rules

Style Code

Even a speakeasy has its unspoken rules. Follow these to ensure you look the part when you whisper the password.

The Bee's Knees

  • Commit to the era — the more authentic, the more fun
  • Layer with vests, suspenders, stoles, and long gloves
  • Embrace metallics — gold, silver, and bronze catch the light beautifully
  • Accessorize boldly: headbands, pearls, pocket watches, brooches
  • Dance-friendly shoes — you'll be Charlestoning all night
  • Dark, moody makeup: berry lips and smokey eyes
  • A spritz of something intoxicating — perfume counts as fashion

The Cat's Frown

  • No jeans, sneakers, or athleisure — the bouncer will notice
  • Avoid modern prints like camo, tropical, or neon patterns
  • Skip the plastic costume-shop accessories — aim for quality
  • No bright colors — stick to the moody, rich palette
  • Don't over-modernize: leave the smartwatch at home
  • Avoid anything too casual — this is a soirée, not a saloon
